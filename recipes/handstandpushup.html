Handstand Push Up

About

An advanced calisthenics move that looks cool and will attract many compliments and envy amongst gymbros & gymgals.

Muscles Involved

Triceps, deltoids, trapezius, latissimus dorsi, pectorals, forearm stabilisers (wrist flexors/extensors), core/abdominals, spinal extensors and glutes.

Key Components and Progressions

  • Push Ups

    1. Floor push ups
    2. Pike push ups
    3. Feet elevated box push ups
    4. Wall assisted handstand push ups (back to wall)
    5. Wall assisted handstand push ups (chest to wall)
    6. Freestanding handstand eccentric handstand push up
    7. Headstand push up to handstand

  • Handstand

    1. Wall assisted handstand
    2. Freestanding handstand hold (including straight variation)
    3. Build up freestanding handstand hold time (aim for solid 30-60 seconds)

FAQ

How long will it take me to get it?

It varies hugely depending on many factors including your general build, baseline abilities, training regime, etc. If you are currently unable to hold a freestanding handstand then it will likely take 12-24+ months of consistent training to achieve a clean repetition.

Any tips?

Focus on getting a solid handstand hold. You will naturally improve your bent arm strength by continuously saving your handstand falls. Slow eccentrics and time held under tension will help a lot. Place your hands slightly wider than your normal handstand position to increase your base of support. Try to keep your forearms vertical to the ground. When lowering your head position should come forward infront of your hands. Record yourself regularly to check your form and correct as required. Be patient and consistent, you will get it.

I can do it, now what?

Progressions. Increase range of motion by doing it on a step/ledge/blocks. Build up repetitions. Increase resistance by using a weighed vest. Plyometrics - jumping handstand push ups, clapping handstand push ups. Achieve god level one arm handstand push ups (good luck).
